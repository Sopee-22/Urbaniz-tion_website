Urbanization

By Sophie Alarilla, Lucas Magdaraog, Ryan Duculan,Akiro Lim, and Sam Ulep

What is Urbanization?

Urbanization is when many people move to an urban area or city. It causes cities to overpopulate. This problem affects many countries such as the US, Chile, Mexico, and more.

What causes Urbanization?

Urbanization is usually caused by urbanized cities having more opportunities for people who live in rural areas. This conflict also usually happens when a country is still growing.

How does it affect us?

Victims of Urbanization include...

  • The people

  • Animals

  • Environment

  • The Earth

  • The people:

    • You might be wondering "how does it affect us as people?". It affects us because, if the city is crowded people would spread diseases easily, which is unhygienic. It could also lead to high unemployment and poverty rate because there will not be enough job openings for everyone who lives in the city. This can lead to poverty because, without a job, people won't be able to earn money to provide themselves and their families the essentials (food, water, shelter, etc). Lastly, to make space for those in the cities, the government will have to convert forests or other green areas. This is dangerous for us as there is already a lot of air pollution and cutting down trees will make it worse. If more trees are lost, the air gets more polluted due to smoke from factories and other sources and there will be less trees to produce oxygen and endangering our lungs. The loss of trees will also cause more floods when there is heavy rainfall, and more landslides that could devastate cities.

  • Animals and environment:

    • While the animals and the environment can get affected because when it gets crouded around cities, the government will have to expand the city. This causes a lot of deforestation and habitat loss because, to make more space, the government will have to destroy the natural environment for expansion. Therefore, many animals will lose their homes and habitats, causing them to migrate or get endangered since animals rely on their environment to survive.

  • The Earth:

    • Lastly, Urbanization can affect the Earth as the expanding cities leave no room for the Earth's beauty to thrive. Basically, many of the Earth's natural resources like the animals and plants that live on the planet are being deprioritized by the goverment's attempts to make room for the citizens. The Earth is dying, and this will affect us all, unless something is done.

    What can we do to lessen the effects of Urbanization?

  • Support local businesses- If local business owners see that their businesses are doing well in rural areas, they most likely will not have to transfer to cities because they can support themselves and their families.

  • Plant more trees- If we plant more trees, many of the trees that were lost can be replaced and incorporated in the city's design. For example, in Nuvali, there are many trees in the city which replaces the trees lost during construction.

  • Advocate for Climate action- If we advocate for Climate Action we can stop the effects Urbanized cities have on the Earth. For example, the GHG (greenhouse gases) and the smoke from cars is making our Ozone layer thinner. This causes the Earth's climate to get hotter as the Ozone layer protects the Earth from the Sun. This means we will have to do our best to support Climate action to reduce the effectS of Urbanization and the other conflicts that it causes.

    • Conclusion:

    Urbanization is when people transfer from rural areas to urban areas. This usually happens when a country is still developing and when cities have better opportunities and/or service. This conflict can affect the people, animals, environment, and the Earth. We can help reduce effects of Urbanization by planting trees, promoting Climate Action, and supporting local businesses.
